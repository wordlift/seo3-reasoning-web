What Is Pre-Settlement Funding?

Pre-settlement funding, often referred to by consumers as lawsuit loans, is a non-recourse financing option provided to plaintiffs involved in a legal case. It is an advance against your future settlement, which helps you have the necessary funds to manage your expenses while your case is ongoing.

Unlike traditional loans, the repayment for a pre-settlement advance is contingent on the outcome of your legal case. If you lose, you are not obligated to repay the advance, providing a level of financial security for the borrower.

How Does Pre-Settlement Funding Work?

  1. The plaintiff applies for funding, providing case details and their attorney's information.
  2. The funding company assesses the case's strength, expected settlement amount, and other relevant factors.
  3. If approved, the plaintiff receives an advance of 10-20% of the expected settlement.
  4. When the plaintiff wins or settles, their attorney handles payment from the case proceeds. If they lose, they generally don't repay anything.
